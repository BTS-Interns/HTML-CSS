Margin Collapsing

Vertical Collide

The first block (orange) has a margin-bottom of 25px and the second one (blue) a margin-top of 50px. You'll probably assume that the total space between the two blocks is 75px but is not; Because of the collapsing margins effect, the bigger one (50px) is the one that stays, overlaping the smallest one. The same applies when if they have size, one of them stay even if they're equal.

margin-bottom: 25px

margin-top: 50px

When one of the margins is negative, then it's subtracted. The margin-bottom of -25px substracts the margin-top of the 50px, leting a total space of 25px between the two blocks.

margin-bottom: -25px

margin-top: 50px

When both of the margins are negative, then the smallest one wins (50px). The same rule as if they were positives.

margin-bottom: -25px

margin-top: -50px

Parents and Children Collapsing

In this example there are six nested divs, with a margin of 15px, you'll think that all of them will be separated, but they don't because the parent will override those margins as if they were 0px (top and bottom). And that happens because they don't have a 'solid' element, like a p tag for instance.

Collapsed

To fix this, you can add a padding of 1px (to be solid) for all the elements, and they will be visible now.
